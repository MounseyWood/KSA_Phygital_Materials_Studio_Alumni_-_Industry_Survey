What is Phygital?

The word phygital describes the overlap between the physical and the digital. In fashion, that translates to workflows where handling a fabric, digitising it for modelling in CG3D&I Digital Product Creation software, and annotating it with data for Digital Product Passport intergration aren't separated steps. They're one continuous act of design.

Your Materials Prepared for the Future

We have made a start.

The Phygital Materials Studio student-led team and our Creative Practioners translate physical fabric swatches into structured, accurate, data-rich digital files — including DPC platform ready texture maps, with verified physical properties, and all available sustainability and design affordance metadata; ready for integration into PIM and EU Digital Product Passport documentation.

Our Questions To You
Three Services

Help us understand what the industry actually needs. The survey covers three service areas. You don't need to have views on all of them — skip what isn't relevant to your work — your insight and feedback will help us develop our future plans and funding bids.

Fabric Digitisation — Concierge Service

A professional scan-to-file service for designers and brands who work with and require verified, accurate digital sampling.

Studio Access to Kingston School of Art Phygital Workshops

Access to the Vizoo flatbed scanner, CLO Fabric Kit, and 3D workstations for alumni and industry partners doing their own digital product creation work.

Continuing Professional Development

Short CPD programmes for alumni and industry professionals. We cover: EU Digital Product Passport compliance, which is mandatory for fashion and textiles product from mid-2027. DPC tool CLO3D, which is already industry-standard at many major brands.

Kingston University Knowledge Exchange Programmes.

Kingston School of Art alumni are working across every level of the fashion industry — freelance studios and consultancies, leading Uk and global brands, innovative start-ups and supply chains. That incredible breadth of lived experience is the one thing the University cannot fully generate from within. The Phygital Materials Studio is being developed for the next generation of fashion designers and product specialists. The students who graduate into your industry will be more useful to you arriving knowing how to work with digital materials, DPC platforms and DPP requirements. That's the practical argument for staying connected and being a part of what's being built here.
